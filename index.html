Lesson 1: Angles

An angle is the measure of the space between two lines that intersect. This measurement is in degrees. The measurement of the angle helps to distinguish which type of angle we are dealing with.

  • Acute: the angle is less than 90 degrees.
  • Right: the angle is exactly 90 degrees.
  • Obtuse: the angle is greater than 90 degrees.

Pythagorean Theorem

The pythagorean theorem refers to a formula that helps describe how the sides of a right triangle relate to one another. Most of the instances in basic geometry find you determining the length of the hypotenuse of the triangle. For this, you use the pythagorean theorem.

  • Pythagorean Theorem: a^2 + b^2 = c^2
  • For solving the length of a hypotenuse (or c), you would rearrange the formula to be:
  • c = sqrt(a^2 + b^2)

Lesson 3: Area vs. Perimeter

Area and perimeter are two of the prominent measurements in geometry. They are also easy to get confused, so it is important to learn the difference between them and how to compute each.

  • Area: the measurement of the space within a two-dimensional figure. For simplicity, we will look at a rectangle. The area of a recangle is measured by multiplying the height by the width.
  • Ex) A rectangle with a width of 20 and a height of 10 has an area of 10 x 20 = 200.
  • Perimeter: the measurement of the length of the boundary of a two-dimensional figure. Again, we will use the rectangle as a basic example. The perimeter of a rectangle is 2 x height + 2 x width.
  • Ex) A rectangle with a width of 20 and a height of 10 has a perimeter of 2 x 20 + 2 x 10 = 60.

Lesson 4: Volume vs Surface Area

Much like area and perimeter, volume and surface area are easily confused for students just starting in geometry. It is important to differentiate the two and memorize the formula for each.

  • Volume: the measurement of the space within a three-dimensional figure. For our basic example, we will use a rectangular box. The volume of a rectangular box is length x width x height.
  • Ex) A rectangular box with a width of 20, a length of 20, and a height of 10 has a volume of 20 x 20 x 10 = 4000.
  • Surface Area: the measurement of the area of the surface of a three-dimensional figure. Using a rectangular box, we can find the surface area by calculating 2(width x length + width x height + height x length).
  • Ex) A rectangular box with a width of 20, a length of 20, and a height of 10 has a surface area of 2(20 x 20 + 20 x 10 + 10 x 20) = 1600.

In the visual representation of the examples above, you can see the clear difference in calculation. Splitting the three-dimensional figure into many two-dimensional figures also makes it simpler to understand and calculate surface area, as that calculation is the total of the areas of all the faces that make up the three-dimensional figure.
